In any field, we want to make use of our data. We want to extract useful information
from a dataset. Computational physics mixes physical understanding with numerical
analysis and computer science to efficiently and accurately read data then form 
theories or predictions based on that analysis. While the study of our physical world
may seem distant from the study of computer language, computational physics bridges
the two fields and aids each--we learn more about the possibilities of coding and we use our knowledge to expand that of physics.
