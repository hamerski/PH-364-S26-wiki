In undergrad, I did a little bit of computational quantum chemistry focusing on the band structure of thermoelectric semiconductors.  All of the coding was done through linux using a premade program. Alot of the coding used what we learned about coding in the terminal along with additional data input including atomic number, position, lattice vectors, and atomic size.  A basic understanding of quantum chemistry is needed to understand many electron systems, band theory, and how electrons behave.  The most important concept in my case was understanding band theory, looking for materials with specific  narrow band gaps- the energy gap between the highest occupied orbital and the lowest unoccupied orbital. As for coding, understanding the code is mainly creating an moving through directories (with the additional data insertion) helped me understand what was happening inside the computer.
